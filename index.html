DISCOVERY STARTS HERE

Welcome to the Maverick Rover Team at the University of Texas at Arlington!

At the forefront of innovation, the Maverick Rover Team is a multidisciplinary group of students dedicated to engineering the future of space exploration. Our team designs and builds cutting-edge rovers to compete in international competitions such as the University Rover Challenge.
Comprised of students from engineering, science, and design backgrounds, our team works collaboratively to push the boundaries of what's possible in rover technology. Each discipline contributes its unique expertise to the design, programming, and fabrication of rovers that can endure and operate in the harsh conditions of extraterrestrial environments. From mechanical engineers perfecting the suspension systems to computer scientists programming autonomous navigation, every aspect of the rover is meticulously developed.

Through our work, we aim to inspire the next generation of engineers and scientists while contributing to the broader goals of space exploration. The diversity of skills within our team ensures that we tackle challenges from multiple angles, leading to innovative solutions that set our rovers apart in international competitions. Whether it's through competing on the international stage or engaging with the local community, the Maverick Rover Team is always striving to go above and beyond.

Join us as we engineer the future, one rover at a time!

OUR PROGRESS

Shown here is the V1 of our Chassis and Suspension design, it features an aluminum extrusion frame and aluminum sheet metal skin, allowing for a cost effective and easily modifiable chassis. The six-wheeled rocker bogie suspension uses a differential bar to ensure equal movement between the sides, with the two black gas shocks and all rubber airless tires absorbing impact forces.
